1 Appendix 1. Data, Parameter, and Control File Requirements by Process Option {#appendix_1_data_and_param_requirements}
2 ============================================================================================================================

5 
6 SWB 2.0 was designed so that parameters may be supplied to the model
7 on
8 an as-needed basis. The original SWB 1.0 input requirements grew ever
9 larger as more possible calculations methods were added. For this
10 reason, we created a flexible table-based format that allows for
11 parameters to be supplied in any order convenient to the user.
12 
13 For table-based parameter entry, the crucial detail is to enter the
14 proper parameter name in the header of the file. Case does not matter
15 for these heading entries: "DEPLETION_FRACTION" will work as well as
16 "depletion_fraction" or "Depletion_Fraction". For some modules,
17 multiple heading values are recognized as equivalent to one another.
18 For
19 example, to identify a particular table column as holding landuse /
20 land
21 cover codes, SWB 2.0 recognizes any of the following: "LU_Code",
22 "Landuse_Code", or "Landuse Lookup Code". Note also that SWB will
23 fill
24 any blank spaces in the header with underscores before evaluating the
25 values therein. Thus, "Landuse Lookup Code" will be treated as
26 "Landuse_Lookup_Code" by SWB. The idea is that whatever
27 identification
28 makes sense to the modeler should be recognized by SWB and acted upon.
29 
30 This section describes in detail the data, parameter, and control file
31 requirements for each module currently implemented in the SWB 2.0 code.
32

177 Process: Soil Storage Maximum / Plant Available Water
178 -----------------------------------------------------
179 
180 The original way to parameterize the total volume of soil moisture
181 storage (or plant available water) was to specify an available water
182 capacity grid, plus a set of effective plant rooting depths in the
183 lookup table. SWB would multiply these two values to come up with the
184 size of the soil storage reservoir.
185 
186 In some cases it may be useful to calculate the size of the soil
187 moisture reservoir outside of the SWB framework. This may be
188 accomplished by specifying that the soil storage maximum will be read
189 into SWB from an external grid file. *Specifying the soil storage
190 maximum this way will cause the rooting depths and available water
191 capacity values to be ignored.*

223 Process: Runoff from Impervious surfaces
224 ----------------------------------------
225 
226 Runoff from impervious surfaces may be simulated in a more detailed
227 manner by including a gridded dataset defining the proportion of each
228 gridcell that is comprised of impervious materials. Data may be
229 supplied
230 as either a fraction (0.0-1.0) or percentage (0-100%) of either
231 pervious
232 or *im*pervious surface area.

234 Process: Crop Coefficients (FAO-56)
235 -----------------------------------
236 
237 This module handles all processes associated with simulating the
238 growth
239 and senescence of plants and their effect on soil moisture. There are
240 three ways in which crop coefficient curves may be specified; each
241 landuse code may use one of the three methods. The three methods that
242 may be used to specify the crop coefficient curves are:
243 
244 1. Time-based: specified in terms of the number of days that have
245  elapsed since planting;
246 2. Growing degree-day based: specified in terms of the number of
247  growing degree-days that have passed since planting;
248 3. Monthly: specific crop coefficients may be supplied with a single
249  value per month.
250 
251 *Control File Entry*
252 
253  CROP_COEFFICIENT_METHOD FAO-56

306 Process: Direct Additions
307 -------------------------
308 
309 External estimates for important components of the water budget may be
310 supplied as supplemental grids or as table values. These additional
311 water sources may be applied to the soil storage reservoir or added
312 directly as potential recharge (deep percolation).
313 
314 For both direct addition types, either gridded or table data may be
315 supplied. SWB will always look for gridded data first. If no gridded
316 data are found, SWB will look for table values. Table values are
317 expected to correspond to the landuse codes contained in the main
318 landuse grid.
319
