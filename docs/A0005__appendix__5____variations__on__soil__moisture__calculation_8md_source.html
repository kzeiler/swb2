6 SWB has evolved over time, incorporating additional and improved
7 process
8 formulations whenever possible. This is how SWB and SWB 2.0 ended up
9 with no less than three ways by which to update soil moisture values
10 by
11 means of the Thornthwaite-Mather approach
12 (Thornthwaite, 1948; Thornthwaite and Mather, 1955; Thornthwaite and
13 Mather, 1957).
14 The direct solution method is now the method of choice; the other two
15 methods are included here to document the functioning of earlier
16 versions of the code.
17 
18 Table Values
19 ------------
20 
21 The original version of the SWB model reads in digitized versions of
22 the
23 Thornthwaite-Mather soil-moisture retention tables and follows the
24 original instructions for calculation faithfully. In order to track
25 changes in soil moisture, several intermediary values are calculated,
26 including precipitation minus potential evapotranspiration ($P-PE$),
27 accumulated potential water loss ($APWL$), actual evapotranspiration,
28 soil-moisture surplus, and soil-moisture deficit. These terms are
29 described below.
30 
31 *P minus PE* $(P-PE)$. The first step in calculating a new soil
32 moisture
33 value for any given grid cell is to subtract potential
34 evapotranspiration from the daily precipitation ($P-PE$). Negative
35 values of $P-PE$ represent a potential deficiency of water, whereas
36 positive $P-PE$ values represent a potential surplus of water.
37 
38 *Soil moisture*, $\\Delta\\,soil\\,moisture$. The soil-moisture term
39 represents the amount of water held in soil storage for a given grid
40 cell. Soil moisture has an upper bound that corresponds to the soil's
41 maximum water-holding capacity (roughly equivalent to the field
42 capacity); soil moisture has a lower bound that corresponds to the
43 soil's permanent wilting point.
44 
45 When $P-PE$ is positive, the new soil-moisture value is found by
46 adding
47 this $P-PE$ term directly to the preceding soil-moisture value. If the
48 new soil-moisture value is still below the maximum water-holding
49 capacity, the Thornthwaite-Mather soil-moisture tables are consulted
50 to
51 back-calculate a new, reduced accumulated potential water-loss value.
52 If
53 the new soil-moisture value exceeds the maximum water-holding
54 capacity,
55 the soil-moisture value is capped at the value of the maximum
56 water-holding capacity, the excess moisture is converted to recharge,
57 and the accumulated potential water-loss term is reset to zero.
58 
59 When $P-PE$ is negative, the new soil-moisture term is found by
60 looking
61 up the soil-moisture value associated with the current accumulated
62 potential water-loss value in the Thornthwaite-Mather tables.
63 
64 *Actual ET*. When $P-PE$ is positive, the actual evapotranspiration
65 equals the potential evapotranspiration. When $P-PE$ is negative, the
66 actual evapotranspiration is equal only to the amount of water that
67 can
68 be extracted from the soil ($\\Delta$ soil moisture).

97 Fitted Equations
98 ----------------
99 
100 The amount of computing involved in negotiating the lookup tables and
101 interpolating a result was significant enough to warrant
102 generalization;
103 in addition, small roundoff errors were accumulated in the course of
104 repeated conversions between accumulated potential water loss and the
105 corresponding soil moisture values. In order to avoid the use of
106 lookup
107 tables altogether a generalized equation was developed, using the
108 Thornthwaite and Mather (1957) table values
109 as the basis for the equations.
110 
111 Two equations were fitted and implemented in the SWB 1.0 code. The
112 first
113 equation relates the current soil moisture to an equivalent
114 accumulated
115 potential water loss +eq. 1:
116 
117 $$soil\\,moisture = {10^{({{\\log }_{10}}\\theta }}^{ - APWL\\, \\cdot
118 \\,0.4788{\\kern 1pt} {\\theta ^{ - 1.037}})}\\qquad(1)$$
119 
120 The second equation is used to back-calculate the equivalent
121 accumulated
122 potential water loss value for a given soil moisture amount:
123 
124 $$APWL = {\\log_{10}}({\\theta_{fc}}) - {\\log_{10}}\\left(
125 {\\frac{\\theta }{{\\left( {0.4788\\,{\\theta_{fc}}^{ - 1.037}}
126 \\right)}}} \\right)\\qquad(2)$$
