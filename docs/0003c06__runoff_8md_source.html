6 Soil Conservation Service Curve Number {#curve_number}
7 ========================================================
8 
9 The curve number method defines runoff in relation to the difference
10 between precipitation and an initial abstraction term. Conceptually,
11 this initial abstraction term represents the summation of all
12 processes
13 that might act to reduce runoff, including interception by plants and
14 fallen leaves, depression storage, and infiltration
15 [(Woodward and others, 2003). Equation 2 is used to calculate runoff
16 volumes (Woodward and others, 2002):
17 
18 $$R = \\dfrac{(P - I_a)^2}{(P + [S_{max} - I_a])}\\qquad(1)$$
19 
20 where $R$ is runoff, $P$ is daily precipitation, $S_{max}$ is the
21 maximum soil-moisture holding capacity, and $I_a$ is initial
22 abstraction, the amount of precipitation that must fall before runoff
23 is
24 generated.
25 
26 The initial abstraction ($I_a$) term is related to a maximum storage
27 term ($S_{max}$) as follows:
28 
29 $$I_a = 0.2 S_{max}$$
30 
31 The maximum storage term is defined by the curve number for the
32 land-cover type under consideration:
33 
34 $$S_{max} = \\left( \\dfrac{1000}{CN}\\right) - 10$$
35 
36 Curve numbers are adjusted upward or downward depending on how much
37 precipitation has occurred in the previous 5-day period. The amount of
38 precipitation that has fallen in the previous 5-day period is used to
39 describe soil-moisture conditions; three classes of moisture
40 conditions
41 are defined and are called antecedent runoff condition I, II, and III,
42 defined as shown in table \#.
43 
44 Runoff condition number Description Nongrowing Season Growing Season
45 ------------------------- ----------------- -------------------
46 ----------------
47 I Dry 0.05 1.4
48 II Average 0.5 -- 1.1 1.4 -- 2.1
49 III Near Saturation 1.1 2.1
