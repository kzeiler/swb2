11 Description of Maui
12 ===================
13 
14 The Island of Maui has an area of about 728 square miles
15 ($\\text{mi}^2$)
16 and is the second largest island in the Hawaiian archipelago. Maui is
17 composed of two shield volcanoes. The older volcano, the West Maui
18 Mountain, rises to an altitude of 5,788 ft at Pu‘u Kukui, and the
19 younger volcano, the East Maui Volcano (commonly referred to as
20 Haleakalā), rises to an altitude of 10,023 ft at Pu‘u ‘Ula‘ula (Red
21 Hill). The two volcanoes are connected by an isthmus that is covered
22 with terrestrial and marine sedimentary deposits that are as much as 5
23 miles wide (Stearns and Macdonald, 1942). Erosion of the West Maui
24 Mountain
25 has carved deep valleys and sharp-crested ridges that radiate from
26 near
27 the summit. On Haleakalā, the rainy eastern slope has valleys that are
28 separated by broad areas and ridges. The drier western slope of
29 Haleakalā is less incised and retains the broad, shield shape of the
30 volcano.
31 
32 Steep gradients in mean annual rainfall patterns on Maui reflect the
33 influence of persistent trade winds and orographic rainfall
34 (Giambelluca and others, 2013). On an island-wide basis, mean rainfall
35 on
36 Maui is about 81 inches per year (in/yr). Mean rainfall is more than
37 360
38 in/yr at Pu‘u Kukui. About 5 mi southwest of Pu‘u Kukui, mean rainfall
39 is less than 15 in/yr. Mean rainfall exceeds 100 in/yr for much of the
40 interior uplands of the West Maui Mountain. On Haleakalā, mean
41 rainfall
42 exceeds 200 in/yr on mid-altitude windward slopes. At a rain gage (not
43 shown) near 5,400 ft altitude on windward Haleakalā, mean rainfall is
44 about 404 in/yr, which is among the highest values in the Hawaiian
45 Islands and the world during 1978–2007 (Giambelluca and others, 2013).
46 Leeward slopes in the rain shadow of Haleakalā are much drier. Mean
47 rainfall is less than 25 in/yr for most leeward areas along the
48 coastline and the isthmus. The summit area of Haleakalā is also
49 relatively dry, with mean rainfall between about 35 and 50 in/yr.

51 Application of the Models
52 =========================
53 
54 Gridded SWB 2.0 model input was generated using the polygon-based
55 model
56 input for the Hawaii Water Budget code used in Johnson and others
57 (2014). The model-grid size for SWB 2.0 is 25 meters
58 by 25 meters. For both models, the monthly rainfall time series used
59 is
60 1978–2007 and the land cover is representative of 2010.
