6 No Routing {#routing_none}
7 ============================
8 
9 This process option is self-explanatory: when active, all runoff
10 generated within a cell is assumed to reach an unmodeled surface water
11 feature and then exit the model domain.

13 Downhill Routing {#routing_downhill}
14 ======================================
15 
16 Activation of the overland flow-routing module within SWB allows
17 runoff
18 from one or more cells to become runon to downslope cells. All runoff
19 from a cell is assumed to infiltrate in downslope cells or be routed
20 out
21 of the model domain on the same day in which it originated as rainfall
22 or snowmelt.
23 
24 During model initialization, SWB examines the connectivity between
25 each
26 active cell. On the basis of this connectivity, SWB creates a master
27 list of cell IDs and sorts them from upslope to downslope. When the
28 model solution is calculated each day, the code begins with the cell
29 furthest upslope, performs all mass-balance calculations, and then
30 proceeds to perform the same calculation on the next cell in the list.
31 
32 Connectivity is defined on the basis of an input D8 flow-direction
33 grid;
34 this is a scheme by which connections between cells are encoded as an
35 integer value within the flow-direction grid
36 (O’Callaghan and Mark, 1984). Fig. **??** shows the direction
37 encoding;
38 the arrow shown in the figure shows a hydrologic connection from the
39 central cell to the cell immediately to the southwest of the central
40 cell, resulting in a flow direction value of 8. ![D8 flow direction
41 grid
42 encoding. file:
43 D8_flow_direction.png](images/D8_flow_direction.png){#fig:D8_flow_direction
44 width="2.5in"}
45 
46 Once water is routed to a closed surface depression and
47 evapotranspiration and soil-moisture demands are met, the only loss
48 mechanism is recharge. This results in cases where maximum recharge
49 values of hundreds or thousands of inches per year are calculated.
50 These
51 extremely high values are unrealistic and likely result from surface
52 storage of water not being accounted for. The code described here
53 allows
54 the user to enter a maximum recharge rate for each land-cover and
55 soil-group combination. This feature offers a way to restrict the
56 estimated recharge values to a more reasonable range; however, the
57 rejected recharge is nonetheless removed from the model domain on the
58 same day in which it originated as precipitation or snowmelt.
