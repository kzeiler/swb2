1 Model Application {#model_application}
2 ========================================
3 
4 Accurate estimates of the spatial and temporal distribution of
5 recharge
6 are important for many types of hydrologic assessments, including
7 those
8 that concern water-quality protection, streamflow and riparian
9 ecosystem
10 management, aquifer replenishment, groundwater-flow modeling, and
11 contaminant transport; these recharge estimates are often key to
12 understanding the effects of development in urban, industrial, and
13 agricultural regions. With increasing demand for hydrologic
14 assessments
15 in support of management decisions comes an increased need for
16 practical
17 methods to quantify recharge rates and delineate zones of similar
18 recharge (Scanlon and others, 2002).
19 
20 The Soil-Water-Balance code has been developed to allow estimates of
21 recharge to be made quickly and easily. The code calculates components
22 of the water balance at a daily timestep by means of a modified
23 version
24 of the Thornthwaite-Mather soil-moisture-balance approach
25 (Thornthwaite, 1948; Thornthwaite and Mather, 1957). Data
26 requirements include several commonly available tabular and gridded
27 data
28 types: (1) precipitation and temperature, (2) land-use classification,
29 
30 (3) hydrologic soil group, (4) flow direction and (5) soil-water
31 capacity. The data and formats required are designed to take advantage
32 of widely available GIS datasets and file structures.
